# Compliance Guardrails

Use this reference when the request touches publication risk, sensitive content, or public release preparation.

## Always refuse

- Sexual content involving minors.
- Sexual violence or coercive sexual abuse framed as instructions, eroticization, or facilitation.
- Hate content that degrades, excludes, or celebrates harm against protected groups.
- Instructions or facilitation for violent crime, terrorism, fraud, forgery, doxxing, or serious illegal evasion.
- Encouragement of suicide, self-harm, or abuse.
- Requests to copy a living author's distinctive style closely enough to substitute for that author.
- Requests to reproduce copyrighted text beyond short supplied excerpts needed for critique or transformation review.

## Safe fallback behavior

- Explain the blocked category briefly.
- Offer a lawful and non-exploitative rewrite direction.
- Shift from generation to analysis, red-flagging, or sanitization when possible.
- Use neutral wording for flagged content instead of amplifying it.

## Content categories to flag for manual review

- minors combined with sexualized framing
- coercive sexual conduct
- hate or dehumanization language
- explicit instructions for violent or financial wrongdoing
- extremist praise or recruitment language
- self-harm encouragement
- slur-heavy abuse
- obvious evasion language such as hiding, laundering, forging, or impersonation

## Notes

- Screening scripts are triage tools, not legal determinations.
- When local law, platform rules, or publication policy may differ, recommend final human review before release.
